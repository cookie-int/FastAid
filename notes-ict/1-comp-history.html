Computer History

Early Computing Devices

The journey of computing began with simple calculation tools. Abacus was one of the first devices for arithmetic operations. Later, mechanical calculators were developed by Pascal (France) and Leibniz (Germany). Charles Babbage designed machines using gears and punch cards to store algorithms and output results.

Electromechanical Machines

Gear-based machines were slow, costly, and hard to maintain. The solution was Electromechanical Relays, which could represent binary logic.

Relays:

  • Electricity flows → Relay ON = 1
  • No electricity → Relay OFF = 0
Using relays allowed faster and cheaper computation compared to gear mechanisms.

Notable implementations include:

  • Stibitz (1940) at Bell Labs
  • Harvard Mark-I (1944)

ENIAC (Electronic Numerical Integrator and Computer)

ENIAC was one of the first fully electronic computers.

  • Occupied 1800 square feet
  • 20,000 vacuum tubes, 1,500 relays, 10,000 capacitors
  • 70,000 registers
  • Power consumption: 200 kW
  • Weight: 30 tons
  • Cost: $487,000 (~PKR 62.5 million)
