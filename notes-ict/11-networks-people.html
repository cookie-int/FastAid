Information System: Networks & People

Introduction

An information system relies not only on hardware and software but also on networks and people to function effectively. Networks enable data transmission, while people interact with the system to input data, make decisions, and analyze information.

Networks

Networks are the infrastructure that connects computers, devices, and users. They allow data to flow between different parts of the system, and between external systems or users.

  • Types of Networks:
    • LAN (Local Area Network): Covers a small area like an office or campus.
    • WAN (Wide Area Network): Connects multiple LANs across cities or countries.
    • Internet: Global network connecting millions of computers worldwide.
  • Transmission Methods:
    • Wired: Fiber optic cables, coaxial cables.
    • Wireless: Radio waves, microwaves, Wi-Fi, mobile networks.
  • Protocols: Set of rules that govern communication, e.g., TCP/IP.

People

People are the most important component of an information system. They interact with the system, provide inputs, and use outputs to make decisions. Feedback from users also helps improve system performance.

  • End Users: Use applications to perform tasks and generate outputs.
  • IT Staff: Manage and maintain hardware, software, and networks.
  • Analysts: Interpret system data to support decision-making.
  • Managers: Use information to plan and oversee operations.

Synergy of Networks and People

The combination of networks and people allows an information system to:

  • Transmit data quickly and reliably across devices and locations.
  • Enable collaboration and communication among users.
  • Support decision-making through timely information access.
  • Continuously improve system functionality via feedback.

Summary

Networks provide the connectivity needed for data flow, while people provide intelligence, decision-making, and feedback. Together, they ensure that an information system is functional, efficient, and useful.
